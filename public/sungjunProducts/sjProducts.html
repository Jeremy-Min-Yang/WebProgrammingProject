About

Abstract art is a form of visual art that breaks away from traditional representation, focusing instead on shapes, colors, and forms to create compositions independent of visual references in the world. Unlike realistic art, which aims to depict objects and scenes as they appear, abstract art emphasizes emotional expression and conceptual ideas. This style often seeks to evoke subjective interpretations and emotional responses from viewers rather than convey a specific image or narrative. Abstract art emerged in the early 20th century, with pioneers like Wassily Kandinsky, Piet Mondrian, and Kazimir Malevich. These artists explored non-representational forms to express inner experiences and philosophical concepts. Abstract art can vary widely, including geometric abstraction, which uses precise shapes and lines, and lyrical abstraction, characterized by fluid, expressive brushstrokes. The goal of abstract art is to provide a visual language that transcends literal interpretation, inviting viewers to engage with the artwork on a deeper, more personal level.
