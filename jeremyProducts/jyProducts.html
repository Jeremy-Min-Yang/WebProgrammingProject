About

Romanticism paintings emerged in the late 18th and early 19th centuries as a reaction against the rationalism of the Enlightenment. These works emphasized emotion, imagination, and the sublime, often depicting dramatic landscapes, heroic figures, and scenes from literature or mythology. Romantic painters sought to evoke powerful emotional responses, exploring themes of individualism, nature's majesty, and the supernatural. Characterized by vibrant colors, dynamic compositions, and expressive brushwork, Romanticism paintings aimed to transport viewers into fantastical realms and stir their souls with a sense of wonder and awe.
